Andrés Bonifacio

The Father of the Philippine Revolution

Timeline of Andrés Bonifacio

Early Life
Andrés Bonifacio was born on November 30, 1863, in Tondo, Manila.
In 1877, He was orphaned and he had to take care of his younger siblings. To do this he quit school and started looking for ways to support his family.
1881 Andres was able to work as a clerk and then a sales agent. Then later on, he became a warehousemen in Tondo.
Mariages
Andres Bonifacio was married twice: first to a certain Monica in Palomar, Tondo. She was Bonifacio's neighbor in Tondo. Monica died of leprosy and they had no recorded children.
In 1892 Bonifacio, a 29-year-old widower married Gregoria De Jesus.
Early Political Activism
In 1892 Bonifacio was one of the founding members of José Rizal's La Liga Filipina.
Katipunan
On the night of July 7, 1892, Bonifacio and others officially "founded" the Katipunan, or in full, Kataas-taasan, Kagalang-galang na Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan ("Highest and Most Respected Society of the Country's Children".
Philippine Revolution
The Spanish authorities confirmed the existence of the Katipunan on August 19, 1896.
On August 30, 1896, Bonifacio personally led an attack on San Juan del Monte to capture the town's powder magazine and water station (which supplied Manila).
In late 1896, Bonifacio, as the recognized overall leader of the revolution, was invited to Cavite province by rebel leaders to mediate between them and unify their efforts.
Bonifacio's Death
On May 10, 1897 Bonifacio was executed by Filipino traitors. Today he is regarded as a National Hero.
